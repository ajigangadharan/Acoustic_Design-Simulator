1. Instructions & Workflow

Geometry tab: Input L x W and FFL-to-Ceiling height, plus FFL-to-Slab (drives the plenum flanking estimate) and the room's intended use.
Envelope tab: Glazing and door area is calculated from actual W x H x Qty and combined with net wall area on an energy-weighted basis.
Structure / Finishes tabs: Wall build-up sets insulation; every finish (wall, ceiling, floor) sets absorption for RT60.

2. Compliance & Standards
  • BS 8233:2014: Internal ambient noise guidance by space type — target dB is set automatically from the Room Type selector (e.g. 30dB Bedroom, 38dB Executive Office, 45dB Open Office).
  • ISO 16283-1:2014 / ISO 717-1: Field measurement of airborne sound insulation and Rw rating (supersedes the withdrawn ISO 140 series).
  • UAE / Regional Codes: Environmental noise limits (55dB Day/45dB Night) and healthcare/education specific STC requirements.
3. Detailed Disclaimer

Theoretical Precision: This suite uses Sabine's formula (per-surface absorption) and logarithmic/energy-weighted summation for both noise sources and composite transmission loss. Real-world performance is limited by workmanship and installation quality.

Flanking Paths: Noise often bypasses walls through ducts, structural connections, or unsealed partition heads (plenum). This tool estimates these losses, but they must be verified by on-site acoustic testing (ASTM E336).

4. Technical Limitation Notice

Results assume a diffuse sound field. In very small rooms or at low frequencies, "room modes" (standing waves) may occur, causing uneven bass distribution not captured by single-number NRC/Rw ratings. Certified laboratory data from manufacturers is required for material submittals.
